Bitcoin Timeline

  • October 31, 2008 - Bitcoin white paper is published by the creator of Bitcoin, Satoshi Nakamoto.
  • November 8, 2008 - Bitcoin Source Code is made available.
  • January 12, 2009 - The first Bitcoin transaction takes place between Satoshi Nakamoto and developer & cryptographic activist, Hal Finney for 50 BTC.
  • May 22, 2010 - The first real-world transaction takes place when programmer Laszlo Hanyecz offers to pay 10,000 BTC for pizza on the Bitcoin forum, about $25 based on the Bitcoin exchange rate of the time (worth around $5 million today).
  • February 2011 - Silk Road online black market opens.
  • January 28, 2011 - 25% of total Bitcoin has been mined (2.5 million Bitcoin)
  • June 19, 2011 - MtGox exchange hack
  • April 10, 2013 - The first Bitcoin bubble where the price reached an all-time high of $266/BTC from only $13/BTC the prior year.
  • May 2, 2013 - First ever Bitcoin ATM established in California.
  • January 9, 2014 - Overstock begins accepting Bitcoin.
  • May 4, 2015 - The Bitcoin Investment Trust's GBTC begins trading on public markets.
  • July 9, 2016 - Bitcoin reward reduced by half for Miners, initially from 50 BTC to 25 BTC in late 2012, to 12.5 BTC in 2016.

Notable Milestones

Market Cap
On November 6, 2010, the Bitcoin market cap exceeded $1 million USD based on MtGox's exchange rate, where it was being traded at $0.50/BTC at the time. Three years later, Bitcoins market cap surpasses $1 billion on March 28, 2013. Today, Bitcoins ecosystem is continuing to grow and expand at a healthy rate where the market cap is sitting at around $9.5 billion. The market cap and daily volume is trackable in real time.
Value
Bitcoin has certainly had quite the journey. From starting out at only a few cents per Bitcoin, to $100 per Bitcoin in April 2013, it shows that Bitcoin is not just some magic internet money, but rather, something of real-world value. A day after the first U.S. Congressional hearing titled "Beyond Silk Road: Potential Risks, Threats, and Promises of Virtual Currencies" , the price of Bitcoin set a record all-time high at $1,242/BTC on November 19, 2013.
Declared Best Performing Currency of 2015
Using the benchmark of the U.S. Dollar index, currencie all over the world were compared and analyzed on their performance for the year. While the US Dollar was only up by around 8%, Bitcoin ended the year with an increase in 35% value declaring itself the winner over other the currencies of nations such as Israel, Japan, Switzerland, China, India, and Great Britain.
Mainstream Attention
Bitcoin has began gaining traction in the mainstream due to many new merchants, start-ups, and new technologies, that are either vocal about their support for Bitcoin or even implement Bitcoin as a payment method. Even big corporations and businesses are pouring hundreds of millions of dollars into Bitcoin Venture Capital reaffirming Bitcoins healthy state.

Since its birth, Bitcoin has had an undeniably bumpy ride for a range or reasons such as its volatility, use for illegal activity or hacks on online exchanges. Although, Bitcoin itself cannot be hacked, there have been exchanges that have had Bitcoin stolen from them due to vulernablities on the exchanges exploited by hackers. Even after constantly being criticized, Bitcoin still stands.

Although we are not yet at the point where Bitcoin has a huge impact on our lives as the Internet has for us, we will eventually reach that stage. Bitcoins technology falls under the Diffusion of Innovation theory comprised of the stages: Innovation, Adopters, Communication Channels, Time, and Social system. Currently, we are in between the Adpoters and Communcation Channels stage where individuals and businesses are beginning to understand and adopt Bitcoin.

Founder & CEO of Bitcoin hardware wallet company, Case Wallet Inc., Melanie Shapiro expressed in an interview with Huffington Post:

"The math and technology behind the Blockchain is difficult for most to understand, but the beauty is - you don't have to get it to use it. Do you really get how the internet works? TLS/SSL? DNS? HTTPS?
If you ask the average person on the street what HTTPS is or DNS, they likely wouldn't be able to tell you. But these things are underneath most of the applications they use on a daily basis. I think the real success of Bitcoin will be when it's the foundational layer on top of which applications and processes are built without people having to understand what that means."
